City Of Muntinlupa

There are at least three variant spellings of the name "Muntinlupa." Muntinlupa gets its name from the thin top soil in the area. Second, in the 16th century, inhabitants purportedly replied Monte sa Lupa to an inquiry about the name of their town from Spaniards, mistaking the question for the name of the card game they were playing. Third, the nature of the area led to the term Monte, which means mountain, being expanded to Muntinlupa, which means mountain land.

According to some papers, the first and third forms of Muntinlupa and Monte sa Lupa became Muntinlupa as a result of misspelling or mispronunciation. However, no definitive evidence exists to date to determine which of the three stories is historically correct.

Muntinlupa is an old term, which refers to a village now known as the Poblacion. As early as 1800, the Catholic priest Father Joaquin de Zuniga chronicled it as La Poblacion que Sigue se Llama Muntinlupa.

Muntinlupa was under the Augustinians' religious care as early as 1601. The citizens' religious instruction was provided by the Guadalupe Sanctuary convent until one Quijano Bustamante purchased the territory of Muntinlupa from the residents. The Civil Government authorized the proposal of constructing a town out of Muntinlupa's barrios in 1869.
